Why Use Templates?

Templates save time, maintain brand consistency, and make it easy for your team to create professional emails without starting from scratch every time.

üí° Pro Tip: Create templates for your most common email types: newsletters, promotions, announcements, and transactional emails.

Creating a Template

  1. Go to Campaigns ‚Üí Templates
  2. Click "Create New Template"
  3. Give your template a descriptive name
  4. Design your template using the email editor
  5. Add placeholder text where content will change
  6. Click "Save Template"

Template Best Practices

  • Header: Include your logo and navigation
  • Hero section: Main message area with image
  • Body: Flexible content area
  • Call-to-action: Clear button or link
  • Footer: Contact info, social links, unsubscribe

Using Placeholders

Add placeholders for content that changes:

Example template structure:

[HEADLINE GOES HERE]

[Main content paragraph. Replace this with your campaign-specific message.]

[Additional content as needed]

[BUTTON TEXT]

Template Types to Create

Newsletter Template

Multiple content blocks for articles and updates

Promotional Template

Bold headlines, product images, strong CTAs

Announcement Template

Clean, focused on single message

Drip Sequence Template

Consistent format for automated series

Using a Template in a Campaign

  1. Create a new campaign
  2. In the email design step, click "Use Template"
  3. Browse and select your template
  4. The template loads into the editor
  5. Replace placeholder text with your actual content
  6. Customize colors, images, or text as needed

Managing Templates

  • Edit: Update template design and save changes
  • Duplicate: Create variations of existing templates
  • Delete: Remove unused templates
  • Organize: Use folders or tags to categorize
  • Share: Make templates available to team members

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Changes to templates don't affect campaigns that already used that template. Each campaign saves its own copy.

Template Design Tips

  • Keep layouts simple and mobile-friendly
  • Use a single-column design for better mobile display
  • Include your brand colors and fonts
  • Add social media icons in the footer
  • Make unsubscribe link easy to find
  • Test templates across different email clients
  • Leave white space for breathing room

Maintaining Brand Consistency

Use templates to ensure every email reflects your brand:

  • Same logo placement in every email
  • Consistent color scheme
  • Standard font choices
  • Similar button styles
  • Unified voice and tone in placeholder text
